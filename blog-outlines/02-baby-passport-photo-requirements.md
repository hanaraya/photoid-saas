### Section 2: Official Requirements for Baby Passport Photos (500 words)
**H2: US Baby Passport Photo Requirements (Official Specs)**

#### Size & Dimensions (Same as adults)
- 2x2 inches (51x51mm)
- Head size: 1-1 3/8 inches
- Must show full face

#### Special Exceptions for Babies
- **Eyes:** Can be closed for newborns under 6 months
- **Expression:** Neutral expression preferred, but not required for infants
- **Head support:** Parent's hand may appear if supporting head (must not be visible in final crop)
- **Background:** White or off-white (same as adults)
- **Recency:** Within last 6 months

#### What's NOT Allowed
- No other people in the photo (even hands, if possible)
- No pacifiers
- No toys or objects
- No hats (unless religious)
- No white clothing on white background

### Section 3: Age-Specific Tips (400 words)
**H2: Tips by Age Group**

#### Newborns (0-3 months)
- Best time: During sleep or right after feeding
- Lay baby on white sheet
- Shoot from directly above
- Eyes can be closed
- Use natural light from window

#### Infants (3-12 months)
- Sit baby in high chair with white backdrop
- Use toys/distractions (remove before photo)
- Time it when baby is alert and calm
- Take 20+ shots rapidly

#### Toddlers (1-2 years)
- Make it a game
- Use their favorite toy as focus point
- Bribery works (small treats)
- Short attention span = work fast

### Section 4: Step-by-Step Photo Guide (400 words)
**H2: How to Take a Baby Passport Photo at Home**

#### Step 1: Timing is Everything
- After nap + feeding = happy baby
- Avoid hungry/tired times
- Have everything ready BEFORE waking baby

#### Step 2: Setup
- White sheet on bed/floor (newborns)
- White backdrop + high chair (sitting babies)
- Natural window light (face the window)
- Remove distractions from background

#### Step 3: Positioning
- Baby facing camera directly
- No head tilt
- Both ears visible (if possible)
- Full face in frame

#### Step 4: The Photo Shoot
- Work fast (2-3 minutes max)
- Take 30-50 shots minimum
- Check for: eyes open (if >6 months), no blur, neutral expression
- Helper distracts baby while you shoot

#### Step 5: Selecting the Best Shot
- Eyes open and visible?
- No motion blur?
- Face centered?
- Neutral expression (or acceptable for age)?
- No shadows?

### Section 5: Common Mistakes That Get Rejected (300 words)
**H2: 7 Baby Passport Photo Mistakes to Avoid**

1. **Parent's hand visible** — Use white blanket to hide support hand
2. **Closed eyes** — For babies over 6 months, eyes must be open
3. **Wrong expression** — Big smiles, crying, or looking away
4. **Motion blur** — Babies move; use fast shutter speed
5. **White clothing on white background** — Use contrasting colors
6. **Pacifier or toys** — Nothing in mouth or hands
7. **Other people in frame** — Even partial visibility gets rejected

### Section 7: FAQ for Parents (200 words)
**H2: Baby Passport Photo FAQ**

1. **Can my baby's eyes be closed?**
   - Under 6 months: Yes, acceptable
   - Over 6 months: Should be open

2. **Can I hold my baby in the photo?**
   - No, but hand support is allowed if not visible

3. **What if my baby won't stop crying?**
   - Try again later; calm, fed babies photograph better

4. **Can my baby wear a headband or bow?**
   - No, unless for religious purposes

5. **How recent does the photo need to be?**
   - Within 6 months (babies change fast!)
